Profile

About

I hold a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from Universiti Teknologi PETRONAS, an MSc in Petroleum Engineering from Teesside University, and a BSc in Chemical Engineering from the Institute of Chemical Engineering. I have built my expertise across thermodynamics, separations, gas hydrates, CO₂ capture, catalysis, and AI-driven process optimization through academic and industry roles spanning Malaysia, Oman, Pakistan, and the US.

I am currently an Assistant Professor at the University of Nizwa (on leave), and have previously served at the School of Chemical & Material Engineering, NUST. My research focuses on thermodynamic modeling, gas hydrate prediction, catalytic hydrogenation of CO₂ to methanol, plasma-assisted CO₂ conversion, and machine learning for process optimization. I have secured competitive research funding from PETRONAS, The Research Council (TRC) Oman, and YUTP, and published extensively in high-impact peer-reviewed journals.
