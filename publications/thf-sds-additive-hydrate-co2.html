Advanced Materials Research, 2016

The Presence of Additive Mixtures (THF/SDS) on CO₂ Rich Gas Mixture Hydrate Formation and Dissociation Conditions

Qazi Nasir, Bhajan Lal, Lau Kok Keong

Paper

Abstract

This study investigates the effect of tetrahydrofuran (THF) and sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS) additive mixtures on hydrate formation and dissociation conditions for CO₂-rich gas mixtures. Understanding the role of promoters and inhibitors is essential for managing hydrate formation in gas processing operations.

Experiments were conducted at various concentrations of THF/SDS mixtures to determine their impact on hydrate phase equilibria. The results demonstrate that THF acts as a thermodynamic promoter while SDS influences kinetic behavior. The combined effects provide insights for designing optimal hydrate management strategies.
