Enhanced Anaerobic Co-Digestion of Food Waste and Solid Poultry Slaughterhouse Waste Using Fixed Bed Digester: Performance and Energy Recovery

Anwar Ahmad1, Roomana Ghufran2, Qazi Nasir3, Fathima Shahitha1, Mohammed Al-Sibani1, Amal S. Al-Rahbi1

1Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, University of Nizwa, Oman
2Department of Biology, University of Nizwa, Oman
3Department of Chemical & Petrochemical Engineering, University of Nizwa, Oman

Paper

Abstract

The increasing generation of food waste and poultry slaughterhouse waste presents significant environmental challenges. Anaerobic co-digestion offers a sustainable solution for waste treatment while recovering energy in the form of biogas. This study investigates the enhanced anaerobic co-digestion of food waste and solid poultry slaughterhouse waste using a fixed bed digester system.

The experimental study evaluated various mixing ratios of food waste to poultry waste to optimize biogas production and process stability. The fixed bed digester configuration provided enhanced microbial retention and process efficiency compared to conventional systems. Results demonstrated significant improvements in biogas yield and methane content, highlighting the potential of this approach for sustainable waste management and renewable energy recovery.
