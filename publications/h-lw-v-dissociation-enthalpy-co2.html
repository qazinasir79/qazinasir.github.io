Measurement of H–LW–V and Dissociation Enthalpy of Carbon Dioxide Rich Synthetic Natural Gas Mixtures

Khalik M. Sabil, Qazi Nasir, Behzad Partoon, Akbar A. Seman

Paper

Abstract

This study presents experimental measurements of hydrate-liquid water-vapor (H–LW–V) equilibrium conditions and dissociation enthalpies for CO₂-rich synthetic natural gas mixtures. The data obtained are critical for designing and operating natural gas processing facilities where hydrate formation poses flow assurance risks.

Experiments were conducted using an isochoric method over a range of temperatures and pressures. The dissociation enthalpies were calculated using the Clausius-Clapeyron equation. The results provide valuable thermodynamic data for understanding hydrate behavior in CO₂-rich systems and validate predictive models used in gas processing industry.
