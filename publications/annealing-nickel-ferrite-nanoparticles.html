Study of Annealing Conditions on Particle Size of Nickel Ferrite Nanoparticles

Israf Ud Din, S. Tasleem, Abdul Naeem, Maizatul S. Shaharun, Qazi Nasir

Paper

Abstract

This study investigates the effect of annealing temperature and duration on the particle size, crystallinity, and magnetic properties of nickel ferrite nanoparticles synthesized via a wet chemical route. Nickel ferrite nanoparticles have wide applications in catalysis, magnetic data storage, and biomedical devices.

The nanoparticles were synthesized by co-precipitation and annealed at various temperatures (400–800°C) for different durations. Characterization using XRD, SEM, and TEM revealed that annealing conditions significantly influence crystallite size, phase purity, and particle morphology. Optimal conditions were identified for producing nanoparticles with desired properties.
