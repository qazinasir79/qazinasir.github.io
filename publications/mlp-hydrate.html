A Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network Model for Predicting Hydrate Equilibrium Conditions in Multi-Component Hydrocarbon Systems

Qazi Nasir1,2, Humbul Suleman3, Israf Ud Din4, Yasir A. Elfadol1

1Department of Chemical & Petrochemical Engineering, University of Nizwa, Oman
2School of Chemical & Materials Engineering, NUST, Pakistan
3Department of Chemical Engineering, University of Hafr Al Batin, Saudi Arabia
4Department of Chemistry, College of Science, Imam Mohammad Ibn Saud Islamic University, Saudi Arabia

Paper

Abstract

Gas hydrates are crystalline solids that form under low temperature and high pressure conditions, posing significant flow assurance challenges in the oil and gas industry. Accurate prediction of hydrate formation conditions is crucial for designing prevention strategies and ensuring safe operation. This study develops a multi-layer perceptron (MLP) neural network model for predicting hydrate equilibrium conditions in multi-component hydrocarbon systems.

The MLP model was trained and validated using extensive experimental data from literature covering a wide range of gas compositions, pressures, and temperatures. Various network architectures were explored to optimize prediction accuracy. The results demonstrate that the MLP model provides accurate and reliable predictions of hydrate equilibrium conditions, outperforming traditional correlation-based approaches and offering a computationally efficient alternative to thermodynamic modeling.
