Comparative Study of Electrolyte Thermodynamic Models for CO₂ Solubility in Water at High Pressure

Humbul Suleman, Qazi Nasir, Abdulhalim Shah Maulud, Zakaria Man

Paper

Abstract

This study compares several electrolyte thermodynamic models for predicting CO₂ solubility in water at high pressure conditions relevant to carbon capture and storage applications. Accurate modeling of CO₂ solubility in aqueous systems is critical for designing absorption-based CO₂ capture processes.

Four electrolyte models (Pitzer, eNRTL, electrolyte-NRTL, and extended UNIQUAC) were evaluated against experimental data from literature. The models were assessed for their accuracy in predicting CO₂ solubility across a range of temperatures, pressures, and ionic strengths. Recommendations are provided for model selection based on operating conditions.
