Predicting Gas Hydrate Equilibria in Multicomponent Systems with a Machine Learning Approach

Qazi Nasir1, Wameath S. Abdul Majeed1, Humbul Suleman2

1Department of Chemical & Petrochemical Engineering, University of Nizwa, Oman
2Department of Chemical Engineering, University of Hafr Al Batin, Saudi Arabia

Paper

Abstract

Gas hydrate equilibrium conditions in multicomponent systems are critical for flow assurance in natural gas production and transportation. Traditional thermodynamic models, while accurate, are computationally intensive and require detailed knowledge of system parameters. This work presents a machine learning framework for predicting hydrate equilibrium conditions in multicomponent hydrocarbon systems.

The developed models were trained on a comprehensive dataset of hydrate equilibrium data for various gas mixtures. Feature engineering incorporated key thermodynamic parameters and compositional information. The machine learning approach demonstrated excellent predictive accuracy across diverse conditions, providing a robust and efficient alternative to conventional modeling approaches for hydrate equilibrium prediction.
