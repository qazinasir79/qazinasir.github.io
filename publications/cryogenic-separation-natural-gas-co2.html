An Overview of Cryogenic Separation Techniques for Natural Gas with High CO₂ Content

Bhajan Lal, Azmi Mohd Shariff, Hilmi Mukhtar, Qazi Nasir, Ali Qasim

Paper

Abstract

This paper provides a comprehensive overview of cryogenic separation techniques for removing CO₂ from natural gas streams with high CO₂ content. Cryogenic separation offers advantages for bulk CO₂ removal at high pressure, particularly for enhanced oil recovery and carbon sequestration applications.

Various cryogenic processes are reviewed including controlled freeze zone, CryoCell, and anti-sublimation technologies. The paper compares the energy requirements, CO₂ recovery rates, and capital costs of each approach, identifying optimal operating windows for different feed gas compositions and project requirements.
